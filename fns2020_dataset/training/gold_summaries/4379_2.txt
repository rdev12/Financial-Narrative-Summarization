 Highlights
FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS
• Growth in revenue, cash generated from operations and free cash flow**
• Revenue has increased by 0.8% from £348.6 million in 2012 to £351.5 million
• Adjusted EBITDA* has remained in line with the prior year at £56.3 million
• Cash generated from operations has increased from £29.1 million to 
£47.6 million and free cash flow** has increased from £28.6 million to 
£38.6 million
• Operating loss has increased from £13.1 million to £16.8 million principally 
as a result of non-cash amortisation charges of £66.2 million and basic loss
per share has increased accordingly from 3.46 pence to 6.38 pence 
• Basic adjusted EPS*** has reduced by 2.6% from 13.52 pence to 13.17 pence
mainly as a result of increased finance charges 
• Net debt has increased by £3.2 million to £81.2 million, reflecting the
acquisition, integration and change activity undertaken in the year, offset by
free cash flow** of £38.6 million
• New debt facilities of £200.0 million have been put in place, replacing the
Group’s previous facilities of £140.0 million and providing significant
resources to undertake further strategic acquisitions
• Progressive dividend policy announced with proposed maiden full-year
dividend of 4.0 pence per share
*
adjusted EBITDA is operating loss from continuing operations before amortisation,
depreciation, net exceptional operating costs and share-based payment (credits)/costs.
**
free cash flow is cash generated from operations after the purchase of both tangible
and intangible assets, and adjusting for tax, net interest and specific items including
exceptional items and working capital movements directly linked with acquisitions
(refer to page 28).
***
basic adjusted EPS is basic loss per share adjusted for the after-tax effect of
amortisation of acquisition-related intangible assets, share-based payment
(credits)/costs and net exceptional operating costs.
OPERATIONAL HIGHLIGHTS
• Two acquisitions were completed during the year and one further acquisition
post year end, all performing in line with management expectations
• The proportion of Retail customers taking three or more products has
increased to 24% from 22% in the prior year
• Significant improvements to the product portfolio have further reduced
reliance on fixed line network services. Fixed line calls now make up only 
14% of total Group revenue down from 16% in the prior year
